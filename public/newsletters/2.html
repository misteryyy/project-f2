Hello again everyone, and a big welcome to all of our new joiners!

In our last newsletter a few weeks ago, we talked a bit about who we are, what we want to do, and a little bit about how we plan to do it. (For those of you that missed it, it can be found here.) In this, our second newsletter, we’d like to give you a brief overview on how exactly you can use our platform to validate that next great idea of yours. What’s important now, is that you take the first step to explore the cornerstones of our platform: roles, collaboration and the level system.

Let’s get right down to business...

ROLES

We’ve thought long and hard about how roles affect collaboration, and projects in general. We believe that there are six* key personality traits or archetypes that are present in every successful project. We’re particularly excited about this discovery as we feel that we’ve uncovered a process that people employ in any project they undertake, without ever thinking about it. In our search for potential associates, we categorize them based on their skill set. These roles come together to form a cohesive unit which has the potential to create a durable, determined project that can deal with whatever hiccups come its way. The people you choose to work with will be as close to the project as you yourself will be, so choose them wisely. The collaboration process and how it all works will be explained further on.

1 - Starter

The founder of the project. The seeker of solutions to problems, the visionary. The (wo)man with the plan. This person is the heart and soul of the beginning of the project. The starter will begin cobbling together the framework of the project and add on key people throughout the journey to make the idea a reality.

2 - Builder

The architect of the project. There may be five builders, there may be ten, there may only be one. It could be a programmer, a designer, an artist, an engineer, etc. This person is extremely creative, shares your vision, and has the technical chops to help work the idea into a viable product.

3 - Leader

Any group is lost without one. You might be the leader, or you might need someone to take the helm. In either case, this person is essential. When met with difficult decisions, who is going to be the ‘go-to’ person to make sure the right call is made, in a timely fashion? This person is a born communicator who thrives under pressure and can remain focused on the end-goal while maneuvering through all of the obstacles along the way. If and when your project makes it way from theory to reality, this is the person in the best position to begin running the business.

4 - Grower

The marketer of the project. The mouth. The evangelist. This person’s main goal is to get as many people exposed to your fabulous idea and communicate its value by talking everyone’s ear off. As such, (s)he is constantly striving to make things bigger, better, and scalable. This person’s skills as a communicator might even be stronger than the Leader’s, so get them out there shaking hands and kissing babies!

5 - Advisor

The advisor is essential to your project. They may be advising on five other projects based on extensive technical expertise, or focusing all of their attention on your concept to help make sure it reaches its goals. The advisor either has a lot of experience in your field, or a highly specialized technical understanding of your market. The advisor will help to digest all of the crowdsourced feedback your project will receive, and help steer it down the right avenues of development.

*(6) - Investor

The financial backer. This person trades their resources for an agreed upon stake in your project. Their involvement in the operation or development of the project will obviously change on a project-by-project basis, and will ultimately hinge on what you’ve agreed upon within your team. How much control are you prepared to sacrifice to get the project moving?

*While not yet legal, once the rules and regulations of the CROWDFUND Act (an amendment to the JOBS Act) are drafted within the next nine months, we will be ready to implement this as a core functionality of our platform. http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crowd_funding

...but I’m the founder, so how does that work?
As the founder of the project, you may be any or all of these roles. Personally, we doubt very much that you are all five. If you are, then FLO~ will be a lot of fun for you and you will be in high demand. It will be a playground where you are constantly innovating and creating, bravely charging forward, on the vanguard of the big developments within your startup community and beyond - you will be very popular indeed. For polymaths such as yourself, there are other roles available that need to be earned. We will tell you more about those later, so don’t get too far ahead of yourself just yet...

COLLABORATION

So how does this collaboration work? Well, it works like it does in the real world, and indeed, you will need to be meeting in the real world quite a lot. We’ve built something that allows you to help yourself succeed by providing convenient and efficient access to a whole community of people excited to create things just like you. However, we understand that people don’t always click, fit together, gel, meet eye to eye, etc. Like in any relationship, there is a honeymoon period built-in. A time when you’re getting to know each other, getting to know how you work. On FLO~, this period is the time it takes you to progress all the way up to Level 3 (see the following paragraph for a primer on levels). You can accept anyone into your project, (and you can join as many projects as you like) but after that you need to make a choice. If you click on ‘Take Me to Level 3’, then it’s permanent. So, ‘Indiana-Jones-in-the-Last-Crusade’ style, choose wisely. Within that time period, take as much time as you can to get to know the other person, and flesh out as much of your idea as possible. This includes long-term planning, business set-up, pivot opportunities, exit strategies, and any number of future considerations.

LEVELS

Along the way, you will have opportunities to move the project through different levels, all the way from 0 to ‘Validated’. The higher your project is, the closer it is to providing you with an answer to that million-dollar question that got you started in the first place: Is this idea and my planning good enough to be executed successfully? A Validated project has done mountains of work and due diligence to ensure that everything is where it needs to be, and that all the questions and ‘what-ifs’ have answers. A level 1 project on the other hand, is still wet behind the ears and learning the ropes. We’ve left the promotion and movement through these levels completely up to you. We don’t think it’s up to us to tell you where your project is - no one knows that better than you. Having said that, we have put in guidelines and suggestions on what we think should and should not have happened at level x. We believe that our own hard work on FLO~ has given us an acute understanding of what it takes to go through the levels, but feel free to take our suggestions with the proverbial grain of salt.

Level 0 - The FLO~ Box

This is where ideas are born. More than anything else, it functions as a place for our imaginative users to get their proto-ideas out fast. If you’re in a creative flow, or need to get some inspiration through stumbling upon others people’s intriguing ideas when in a creative rut, this is the place to be. While not a crucial part of the project process for some, it’s a space available for those who care to use it.

Level 1 - Red

The ‘putting pen to paper’ phase. You have the beginnings of an idea to build on, but there’s a lot of questions surrounding the practicality, attractiveness, feasibility, market impact etc. of the project. You’re starting to realize the type of people you want on your team, and are starting to seek them out.

Level 2 - Orange

The filtering phase. You’ve gotten some talent on board, you’re validating your core idea, you’ve done some re-evaluating of the idea and started to evaluate potential pivots of your original idea.

Level 3 - Green

The strategic planning phase: considering how to sell it, how to present it, how to execute in order to Validate and hit the ground running toward growth.

Level 4 - Validated

Meatspace. At this point, you have all the tools and information necessary to make a go of it in the real world, with the confidence that comes with knowing you’ve done your due-diligence and built the best team possible, to create the best possible product.
